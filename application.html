Role Structure
Authority & Responsibilities

The Guild operates on a tiered authority structure with three main branches: Administrative (Site Control & Structure), Moderation (Rules & Enforcement), and Member/Contributor (Community & Content Creation). Below is a complete overview of each role and what they can do.

Member & Contributor Branch
Newbie
Level 2
Default state upon discovering the site.
Authority
None — Observational access only.
Responsibility
Read and explore the archive freely.
Site Member
Level 3
Registered user level with basic community access.
Authority
  • Comment on pages
  • Upvote pages and comments
  • Report content
Responsibility
Participate respectfully in community discussions and follow community standards.
Contributor
Level 4
Entry into active participation (must apply). Considered part of the Guild's working body.
Authority
  • Edit registry pages
  • Contribute content
  • Participate in collaborative projects
Responsibility
Create and maintain high-quality archive entries. No authority over users or rule enforcement — purely a content role (author-level).
Moderation Branch
Moderator
Level 4+
Frontline enforcement role focused on rule adherence.
Authority
  • Remove inappropriate content
  • Warn users
  • Escalate serious issues
Responsibility
Monitor community behavior and enforce content standards. Report violations transparently and follow established guidelines.
Senior Moderator
Level 5
Experienced moderation authority with expanded capabilities.
Authority
  • Handle complex disputes
  • Temporarily restrict contributors (up to 2 months)
  • Guide junior moderators
Responsibility
Mentor moderation staff and handle escalations. Ensure consistency in enforcement standards across the team.
Deputy Chief of Moderation
Level 5
Second-in-command of the moderation branch.
Authority
  • Oversee moderator performance
  • Handle escalations
  • Assist Chief of Moderation
  • Promote moderation staff (with authority)
Responsibility
Manage moderation team operations and coordinate enforcement with administration.
Chief of Moderation
Level 5+
Head of all moderation operations and rule enforcement.
Authority
  • Define enforcement standards
  • Handle major violations
  • Coordinate with Administrators
  • Promote moderation staff (with authority)
Responsibility
Establish and maintain moderation policies. Lead the moderation team and ensure fair, consistent enforcement across the Guild.
Administrative Branch
Administrator
Level 5
Core operational staff managing site structure and content approval.
Authority
  • Edit and approve pages
  • Enforce rules
  • Manage users (must include report logs when removing contributors)
Responsibility
Approve content and maintain site structure. Work alongside moderators to ensure stability.
Senior Administrator
Level 5+
Oversees major site sections and administrative teams.
Authority
  • Approve major structural changes
  • Supervise admins and moderation leadership
  • Ban users (with evidence)
  • Demote other admins (cannot demote moderators)
Responsibility
Oversee administrative operations and high-level site decisions. Provide strategic direction for the archive.
Deputy Chief Administrator
Level 6
Second-in-command of the administrative branch.
Authority
  • Handle escalations
  • Act in place of Chief Admin
  • Remove contributors independently
  • Demote moderation ranks below Deputy Chief of Moderation
  • Promote users (with authority)
Responsibility
Manage administrative operations and assist Chief Administrator in site governance.
Chief Administrator
Level 6+
Highest authority below Owner. Full control over system operations.
Authority
  • Final decisions on disputes and bans
  • System-wide policy control
  • Permission management
  • Structural oversight
  • Promote users (with authority)
Responsibility
Oversee all administrative and moderation operations. Set system-wide policies and resolve conflicts at the highest level.

Key Principles
  • Contributors are part of the system but hold no authority over other users
  • Moderation focuses on people and behavior; Administration focuses on content, structure, and permissions
  • Both moderation and administrative branches are equal in importance but operate in different domains
  • Promotions are strictly controlled by designated high-ranking roles
  • Role changes require transparent documentation and adherence to established guidelines
